My time in Iraq involved a lot of guard duty, which is why i have chosen to display these pictures. They were the every day view, for six hours a day. A lot of dust, a lot of rocks, and not a lot of life.

When you are surrounded by nothing but dust and barbed wire in the middle of a desert, you have to rely on your friends and collegues to keep you sane. It doesn't matter if you are flying into a warzone, building artillery safe bunkers, or taking cover in said bunkers.

In the midst of this great adventure, you sometimes have to stop and take a look at what is happening around you. Even though you are in a country riddled with bulletholes and ruined buildings, a spectacular sunset might be presenting itself just around the corner.
